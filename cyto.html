THE CYTOPLASM IS HERE TO HELP
I, Mr. Cyto Pete Plasm, will keep Cell City in its prime




First and foremost, I should introduce myself to the citizens.
Who am I? What am I made of?

I am a jelly-like substance that fills the cell.
I contain dissolved molecular building blocks and organelles.
I hold the floating DNA in prokaryotic cells so they don't fly away!

The cytoskeleton is embedded within me and it's so important to every cell!
It's made of long fiber threads, composed of microtubes, intermediate filaments, and microfilaments.
Microtubes give the cell its shape and allows organelle movement and cell division by pulling the DNA apart.
Intermediate filaments give the cell strength.
Microfilaments enable cell movement and division, which is great for muscle cells.

I am also hold cytosol, which consists mostly of water, THE SPICE OF LIFE.
This is EXTREMELY important and necessary for cell structure
